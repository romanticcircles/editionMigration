LB98-Bbl 1798: Southey’s copy, Bristol (BL) 1798: Robert Southey’s copy of Lyrical Ballads, Bristol imprint. Described sometimes as an 'archival' copy, it contains both S. T. Coleridge’s 'Lewti,' from the first state of the printing (see Reed, A3) and the 'Advertisement' and S. T. Coleridge’s 'The Nightingale' from the second state. British Library.
LB98-BEDDOES 1798: Beddoes's copy, Bristol, with "Domiciliary Verses" (BL) 1798: Thomas Beddoes's copy of Lyrical Ballads, Bristol imprint, second state. British Library (Ashley 2250). As Reed notes, A3, a leaf has been added after p. 62, containing 'Domiciliary Verses,' an apparent parody probably written by Beddoes himself.
lb98-bnypl 1798: Fricker's copy, Bristol, with "Lewti" (NYPL) 1798: Martha Fricker’s copy of Lyrical Ballads, Bristol imprint, first state. With corrections in S. T. Coleridge’s hand. Berg Collection, New York Public Library.

INSCRIPTION
For the Spot where the Hermitagestood on
St. Herbert’s Island, Derwent Water.

IF thou in the dear love of some one friend1Hast been so happy, that thou know’st what thoughts2Will, sometimes, in the happiness of love3Make the heart sink, then wilt thou reverence4This quiet spot.—St. Herbert hither came,5And here, for many seasons, from the world6Remov’d, and the affections of the world,7He dwelt in solitude. He living here,8This island’s sole inhabitant! had left9A Fellow-labourer, whom the good Man lov’d10As his own soul; and when within his cave11Alone he knelt before the Crucifix,12While o’er the lake the cataract of Lodore13Peal’d to his orisons, and when he pac’d14Along the beach of this small isle and thought15Of his Companion, he had pray’d that both16Might die in the same moment. Nor in vain17So pray’d he:—as our Chronicles report,18Though here the Hermit number’d his last days,19Far from St. Cuthbert his beloved friend,20Those holy men both died in the same hour.20

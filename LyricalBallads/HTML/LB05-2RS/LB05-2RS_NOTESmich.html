NOTES TO THE POEM OF MICHAEL.

note i.

Page 224—line 6. “There’s Richard Bateman,” &c. The
story alluded to here is well known in the country. The
chapel is called Ings Chapel; and is on the right hand side
of the road leading from Kendal to Ambleside.

note ii.

Page 227—line 15. “——had designed to build a sheep-
fold.” &c. It may be proper to inform some readers, that
a sheep-fold in these mountains is an unroofed building of
stone walls, with different divisions. It is generally placed
by the side of a brook, for the convenience of washing the
sheep; but it is also useful as a shelter for them, and as a
place to drive them into, to enable the shepherds conveni-
ently to single out one or more for any particular purpose.

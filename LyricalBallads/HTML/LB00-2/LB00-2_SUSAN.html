POOR SUSAN.

At the corner of Wood-Street, when day-light appears,1There’s a Thrush that sings loud, it has sung for three years:2Poor Susan has pass’d by the spot and has heard3In the silence of morning the song of the bird.4

2

’Tis a note of enchantment ; what ails her ? She sees5A mountain ascending, a vision of trees ;6Bright volumes of vapour through Lothbury glide,7And a river flows on through the vale of Cheapside.8

3

Green pastures she views in the midst of the dale,9Down which she so often has tripp’d with her pail,10And a single small cottage, a nest like a dove’s,11The only one dwelling on earth that she loves.12

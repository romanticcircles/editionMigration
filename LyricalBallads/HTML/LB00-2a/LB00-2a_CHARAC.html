A CHARACTER,

In the antithetical Manner.
1

I marvel how Nature could ever find space1For the weight and the levity seen in his face:2There’s thought and no thought, and there’s paleness and bloom,3And bustle and sluggishness, pleasure and gloom.4

2

There’s weakness, and strength both redundant and vain;5Such strength, as if ever affliction and pain6Could pierce through a temper that’s soft to disease,7Would be rational peace—a philosopher’s ease.8

3

There’s indifference, alike when he fails and succeeds,9And attention full ten times as much as there needs,10Pride where there’s no envy, there’s so much of joy;11And mildness, and spirit both forward and coy.12

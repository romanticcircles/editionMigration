NOTES TO THE POEM OF MICHAEL.
note i.

Page 213—line 14 “There’s Richard Bateman,” &c. The
story alluded to here is well known in the country. The
chapel is called Ings Chapel ; and is on the right hand side
of the road leading from Kendal to Ambleside.


note ii.

Page 217—line 4 “———had design’d to build a sheep-
fold.” &c. It may be proper to inform some readers, that a
sheep-fold in these mountains is an unroofed building of stone
walls, with different divisions. It is generally placed by the
side of a brook, for the convenience of washing the sheep;
but it is also useful as a shelter for them, and as a place to
drive them into, to enable the shepherds conveniently to
single out one or more for any particular purpose.
